Reading Discussion: A Friend is Writing

Reading A Friend is Writing by Callum Copley made me think about how writing online feels both personal and performative at the same time. The idea that a friend is writing creates a sense of intimacy, like you are witnessing someone’s thoughts in real time. At the same time, the internet turns that personal act into something public and visible to everyone. It made me reflect on how strange that balance is.

What stood out to me was how writing online can blur the line between genuine expression and self presentation. Even when someone feels honest, they are still aware of being watched. As a college student who posts and reads things online often, I see how easy it is to curate a version of yourself. The reading made me question whether online writing can ever be fully authentic, or if it is always shaped by the awareness of an audience.
